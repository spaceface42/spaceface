Portfolio Template / Graphic Designer

Your Name Here

Replace this with a one-paragraph introduction that says what kind of graphic designer you are, what you are best at, and what clients remember about working with you. A strong version usually names your focus, your visual point of view, and the kinds of projects you want more of.

First-page essentials

  • Your role and specialty: brand identity, editorial, campaign design, packaging, or digital art direction.
  • Your strongest client type: founders, agencies, cultural institutions, fashion, hospitality, or tech.
  • Your positioning sentence: what makes your work feel distinct, not just competent.

Project One / Identity System

Use this slot for a project that proves your taste fast. Add one sentence on the brief, one on your role, and one on the result.

  • Client or project name
  • Scope: identity, print, digital rollout
  • Outcome: launch, recognition, sales, or engagement

Project Two / Campaign or Editorial

Show range here. If your first card is branding, use this one to show layout systems, image direction, art direction, or motion-ready design thinking.

  • Format: campaign, publication, social system, or launch kit
  • Constraint: timeline, audience, or production limit
  • Why it matters: what changed because of the work

Project Three / Personal or Experimental

This slot is useful for showing authorship. Include a self-initiated series, poster system, image-making direction, or concept that reveals your visual instincts.

  • Medium: posters, typography, image systems, or 3D
  • Intent: exploration, research, or aesthetic statement
  • Connection to paid work: what it says about your eye
About

Add a short biography with your base city, years of experience, and the kinds of collaborations you enjoy most.

Services

List only the services you want to be hired for. Good examples: identity systems, art direction, editorial design, campaign visuals, packaging, and presentation design.

Signals

Add trust markers like selected clients, awards, publications, speaking, teaching, or notable collaborators.

Suggested opening copy

I design visual identities, printed matter, and campaign systems for brands that want clarity, character, and a strong point of view. My work combines structured typography, image-led storytelling, and art direction that helps a brand feel memorable across print and digital.

Over the past few years I have worked on launches, refreshes, and editorial systems for clients who care about detail and cultural relevance. I am especially interested in projects where concept and execution need to feel equally sharp.

Questions to answer on page one

  1. What exact kind of graphic designer are you, in one sentence?
  2. What 3 project categories do you most want to attract?
  3. Which 3 proof points make a visitor trust you quickly?
  4. What visual tone should the page communicate: sharp, playful, elegant, or experimental?
