Skeleton.css / Starter

Minimal portfolio shell with sane tokens, breakpoints, and light or dark theming.

This page is the authored demo for skeleton.css. It is intentionally small: one font, one variable system, a few layout primitives, and enough structure to start a clean portfolio or landing page without inheriting the older site styles.

To lock the theme manually, set data-theme="light" or data-theme="dark" on <html>. If you omit it, the stylesheet follows the system preference.

Starter Copy

What the first screen should answer

  • What kind of designer or studio this is.
  • What categories of work you want more of.
  • Why your visual taste feels distinct instead of just competent.
  • How a visitor can contact you in one move.

Breakpoints

Breakpoint rhythm

The stylesheet ships with three practical breakpoints: 40rem, 64rem, and 80rem.

They are meant for small-layout expansion, real desktop layout, and large-canvas spacing, not endless device-specific overrides.

Notes

Suggested workflow

Start by swapping the three placeholder cards for real work. Then replace the headline and first paragraph before touching colors or layout.

If you want a darker look all the time, add data-theme="dark" to the root HTML element and keep the rest of the page unchanged.

Quick checks before shipping:

  • Compress the opening statement to two or three sentences.
  • Use one contact route that is always visible.
  • Keep the first grid focused on the kind of work you want sold.
  • Test at 40, 64, and 80 rem widths.
